Staggering

Calls

"Chase!", "Stagger!": Run down exposed enemy players

One of the most important tactics in a comp game WHEN YOU HOLD THE OBJECTIVE is to increase the time it takes for the enemy team to regroup to contest the objective with numbers.

If you have the objective, and you have numbers, you have the winning conditions.

The way to stagger is to kill enemy players while the bulk of their team is respawning/regrouping.

Example 1: Staggering after winning the objective

You win the objective. 4 of your players are alive, 4 of theirs are dead and 2 are retreating. You have a 4v2 advantage.

It takes 10 seconds to respawn (usually). If you chase down the remaining 2 and kill them with your numbers advantage, it'll take the enemy team at least 10 seconds + the time to get back to the objective AFTER the last kill to contest again.

This can add huge objective time for you.

Example 2: Aggressive staggering

At our level, players will often get impatient and rush the objective instead of waiting to regoup.

Playing aggressive with numbers advantage to pick off these players can also prevent the enemy from quickly regrouping.

You must be careful not to lose players when the enemy team is close to regrouped. You will not have numbers advantage next fight.
