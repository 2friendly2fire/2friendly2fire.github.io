Staggering

Calls

"Chase!", "Stagger!": Run down exposed enemy players.

Maximize your objective time

One of the most important tactics in a comp game WHEN YOU HOLD THE OBJECTIVE is to increase the time it takes for the enemy team to regroup to contest the objective with numbers.

If you have the objective, and you have numbers, you have the winning conditions.

If you stagger the enemy team and they still attack, numbers advantage will probably give you the next fight.

The way to stagger is to kill enemy players while the bulk of their team is respawning/regrouping.

There are two main tactics for staggering the enemy team.

Tactic 1: Staggering after winning the objective

You win the objective. 4 of your players are alive, 4 of theirs are dead and 2 are retreating. You have a 4v2 advantage.

It takes 10 seconds to respawn (usually). Someone calls "chase!" and you kill the two with your numbers advantage, it'll take the enemy team at least 10 seconds + the time to get back to the objective AFTER the last kill to contest again.

This can add huge objective time for you, especially if you continually stagger out-of-position enemy players.

Tactic 2: Aggressive staggering at the edge of the objective

At our level, players will often get impatient and rush the objective instead of waiting to regoup with their team.

Playing aggressive with numbers advantage to pick off these players can prevent the enemy from quickly regrouping.

You must be careful not to lose players when the enemy team is close to full strength. You will not have numbers advantage next fight.
