Contesting the objective

Calls

"Point!": The point is in danger, stay on the point.

"Fight on cart", "Fight on point": Fight on the objective, not somewhere else.

"No free cart": Don't let the enemy team push the cart without a fight.

Winning the objective wins games

Comp games are won by winning the objective. NOT kills, or anything else.

I shit you not, there is no strategy in a comp game that involves letting the other team push a payload, hold a point, or capture a point, without contesting.

Mental focus on the objective

Teams do need to kill the enemy team to win the objective, but keep the objective front of mind, not kills. It'll help orient you in the game.

If the enemy team wants to hold their private parts somewhere that's not the objective, then by god let them. Don't risk getting killed anywhere but around-ish the objective.

No excuses

The only time you should not be contesting is when you're grouping for numbers.

Saying "I can't contest the objective because [anything but we're grouping for numbers]" is essentially wrong. Change heros, discuss a counter, organize an attack. Not contesting the objective is unambiguously losing the game.

You could have a billion kills, but if you chase down your billionth-and-first kill and the enemy caps the point, you lose.
