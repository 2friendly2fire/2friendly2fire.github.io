Not dying

Calls

No specific calls. General communication about positioning is important.

I'm not joking

It sounds dumb, and facetious, but NOT DYING is probably the most important thing anyone can do.

It's also a very simple objective to keep in mind that'll keep you focused on what's important.

Example: Dying on defense

If you're on defense and your team does not die, you're halfway to winning the game. Simple (if unrealistic).

Somone playing aggressive on defence and getting killed means they're usually at fault for losing the point 20 seconds later.

Don't setup your defenses where you can't retreat to cover or rely on your team.

Feeding

People often misunderstand why they're losing a game when someone is dying too much. The cause is usually called 'feeding.'

It's the concept of too many avoidable deaths producing a consistent numbers advantage for the enemy team.

It's fine to die contesting the objective as a team. Dying out of position for no benefit, or while your team is regrouping is generally considered to be feeding. It's bad.

DO. NOT. DUEL.

Comp is won by winning objectives. Not by killing people. Staying alive to contest is (usually) more important than getting a kill.

Don't duel. Newer players often go to fight, and they fight until they die or the enemy player does.

Wrong. This a bad habit, best to get out of.

Back up, use cover, come back to where your healers can heal you. Use a local numbers advantage. Use your tanks for cover.

Play smart and safe and wait for the enemy to get impatient. Kill them with your team when they're out of position.

Caveat: Contest the objective

The above only applies if you're contesting the objective. Not dying and not contesting is also losing. Fight on that cart.

Playing too passive is a thing as well. Tanks lead the way.
