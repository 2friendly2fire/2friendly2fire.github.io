Not Tilting

Overwatch is a tilt-inducing game. Tilting does seem to affect your abipty to play the game, aside from making it unpleasant for your team members.

There are a few things I keep in mind to keep tilt under control.

Overwatch is hard

You lose at least 40% of games. That's just how it is. Can't win 'em all.

Accept that you suck at OW, or that there's lots you could do better. Made a big difference for me.

It's unlikely in a comp game where everyone is communicating, cooperating and coordinating hero picks that you're losing because of your team. Either the other team is straight better, or a few key mistakes were made. Hands down some of them were yours.

Medals 1

Medals are basically useless as an indicator of anything. I fall into this trap too, I get it, but their only use is against your own team.

It's unlikely in most games that anyone is very far behind you in medal count. Wins and close games usually involve the entire team.

Medals 2

Medals don't tell you much about your own performance.

In the Mercy meta, a player that managed to kill the enemy mercy every round and then died - say 5 elims - is hands down the most useful player on the team. A mercy that can never rez effectively removes a notional enemy player every 30 seconds. Plus no healing. All the other medals on that team are basically cleanup.

Medals 3

If you ever say or think "Well I've got x medals" you're actively making yourself worse at the game. Medals only have one use, and that's to throw in someone's face if they criticise you; the "I know you are but what am I?" of Overwatch.

Throwing/giving up

If you tilt out and throw a game, the only certainy is that you lost the game. Unbelievable comebacks are a regular feature of comp, and one of the most fun parts.

Often, a simple hero switch at the near-last minute can turn the tide.
