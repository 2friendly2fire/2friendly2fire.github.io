A game of yards

A comp game is symmetric. If you have to push a payload, the next round will require the enemy to push the payload on the same map. If you're attacking control points one round, the next you'll be defending.

Unlike Quick Play, which is an asymmetric game mode, the team that gets further on the objective will be the winner.

Why is this important?

Don't hail mary

Always remember the flow of an Overwatch game: Contest and regroup, contest and regroup...

You don't need to get right to the end of a payload map, just further than the enemy team. Getting impatient and throwing yourself at the objective is not useful.

Better to get a few more yards than hail mary the end-zone.

Don't assume a loss

A bad phase (defence or offense) does not mean you've lost the match.

It is a regular feature of comp to have a shit round, followed by a stellar round. Never tilt out or assume the loss. I'd go so far as to say 20% of games don't end as you might expect after a disaster of a first round. That's a lot.
