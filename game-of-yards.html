A game of yards

A comp game is symmetric. If you defend the first round, you attack next round.

Unlike Quick Play, the team that gets further on the objective (more payload distance, more ticks on a control point etc.) will be the winner.

Why is this important?

Don't hail mary

Always remember the flow of an Overwatch game: Contest and regroup, contest and regroup...

You don't need to get right to the end of a payload map to win, just further than the enemy team. Getting impatient and throwing yourself at the objective only to die is not useful.

It's better to organize and get a few more yards than hail mary the end zone and get nothing.

Don't assume a loss

A bad round (defence or offense) does not mean you've lost the match.

It is a regular feature of comp to have a bad round, followed by a stellar round. Never tilt-out or assume the loss.

I'd go so far as to say 20% of games don't end as you might expect after a disaster of a first round. That's a lot.
