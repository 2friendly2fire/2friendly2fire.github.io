Not getting staggered

Calls

"Reset": We've lost the fight, everyone retreat and regroup.

"Winnable!": I don't think the fight for the objective is lost, don't worry about getting staggered, keep contesting.

The flip side: Don't let yourself get staggered

If you DO NOT hold the objective, your primary goal is to regroup as quickly as possible to contest the objective again.

Getting killed (when it is avoidable) after "regroup" or "reset" has been called is pretty much the biggest mistake in Overwatch. It's also one of the most common.

If your team is regrouping for the next fight, the only thing you should be doing is backing-the-fuck-up to the team.

If you get killed during this phase, really the only thing you can say is "Damn." There isn't really anything else as useful as staying alive during this phase.

Even kills aren't that useful

If you get a kill and then get killed, that person will get back before you, with objective time ticking down all the while. Not very useful.

Hmmm... be careful.

An advanced tactic is to build ult during this phase, but ... just be careful. Especially if there's a one-shot character out there. Really, just don't.

Maybe we can still win...

This call fits in a few places, but I'll discuss it here.

The "winnable" call means that it's someone's opinion that you should keep contesting the objective; the objective might still be won.

If "winnable" is called, and ntil "regroup" or "reset" is called, anyone alive should be fighting for the objective.

Anyone respawning should be running in to help as fast as possible.

The exceptions

There are three cases for which it is fine to stagger.

First, when your team has the control point on king of the hill. It's fine to go in one at a time to hold the point, your objective % is ticking up. This is unambiguous.

Second, to stall a payload on defense. This is a judgement call, but if you can keep the payload from moving while the enemy's time is ticking down, this is generally a good thing.

Third, to stall the control point on defense in 2CP. This is also a bit of a judgement call. It might be better to hold a few seconds for numbers, but pay close attention to the enemy tick-level.
